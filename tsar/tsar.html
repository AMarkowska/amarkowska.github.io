Tsar vs Havana
2019

A visual identity inspired by creating
a narrative about two artists/star designers/friends in conflict.

It contains a shocking soundscape that builds up the atmosphere, brutalized modular posters, an invitation that explains the story and a cliffhanger of a greeting card.

In this assignment, students were supposed to make-up an alter ego. We took all letters from our names and surnames, mixed them up to form the new ones. Later our classmates gave us a set of brand new characteristics that we had to stick to. I found the idea of making a narrative around our new personalities the most amusing. My alter geo (Tsar Kakao Maswa), according to my classmates in conflict Havana Rabis. It was one of the options to make a branding so I decided that would be the best medium to tell my story. After experiencing and reading all of the elements the viewer pieces out a story about two friends that were separated by their fame.

The end products (flyer, postcard, posters, and an invitation) were printed with risograph which encouraged design to be modular and prepared for speedy reproduction.

The process was a combination of working digitally, starting with 3D models, and reacting to it with printing and drawing on top of the designs. The circle repeated a few times.
