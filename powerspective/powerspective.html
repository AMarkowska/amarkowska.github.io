PowerSpective
2019

A project about how constructions created by humans reflect structures within society. The associations are based on researching amateur and professional architectural photography.

This project is the second part of a bigger assignment. In the first part, I worked together with Aurora Navarro Villacampa on collection of images that became a visual essay on power structures.

I was very fascinated by what sort of perspectives are used in architectural photography. For the first part, we also took our own images and after analyzing both sources; own and found. I realized that perspective often depends on the kind of building we are taking the picture of. The grander the building and the more power it imposes the more of frog perspective we take. It is connected to the amount of space around the building. Correspondingly crowded and poor neighborhoods are regularly photographed from up to down perspective.
